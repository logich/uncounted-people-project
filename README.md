## Resources and Information 
### Definitions And Terms
Dissociation: An unhealthy coping machanism where somebody dissconects from their thoughts, memories, feeling, body, etc. It often feels as if you are watching a movie of your life instead of living it. It can cause memory and time loss if intense enough.
Plurality: Multiple people or alters sharing one body. There are many variants of plurality and the definition is flexible.
Systems: A somewhat stricter definition of plurality, often meaning distinct separate people.
Median: Plurality where alters are less distinct and often seen as in the middle of being plural and a singlet.
Traumagenic:  A system with an origin  of trauma. These systems are typically disordered, but not always.
Endogenic: A sytem with an origin other than trauma. This includes willogenic systems, neurogenic systems, and more.
Syscourse: The debate on whether or not endogenic systems are valid.
DID: Dissociative Identity Disorder, requiring the following symptoms to be diagnosed; 2 or more distinct alters, amnesia between alters, and distress. These must not be better explained by medication or other medical conditions in order to be diagnosed.
OSDD-1a: Other Specified Dissociative Disorder 1a. A dissociative disorder where there is significant amneisia wih a lack of distinct alters.
OSDD-1b:  Other Specified Dissociative Disorder 1b. A dissociative disorder where alters are distinct and there in little to no amniesia.
OSDD-2: Other Specified Dissociative Disorder 2. A dissociative disorder caused by environments of extreme control and classified by intense disturbance in identity.  Not usually considered a plural disorder.
OSDD-3:  Other Specified Dissociative Disorder 3. A dissociative disorder classified by dissociative reactions  to or caused by recent stress or trauma, typically lasting around a month. Not considered a plural disorder.
OSDD-4: Other Specified Dissociative Disorder 4. A dissociative disorder classified by dissociative trances, such as a lack of awareness of surroundings, transient paralysis, and unresponsiveness. Not considered a plural disorder.
Headspace: A place in the mind where alters can go and interact with eachother and the enviorment. Also known as; inner world and wonderland. Not all systems have a headspace. Some singlets may have a headspace.
Alters: Different identities or people in one body. Also known as; parts, headmates, brainmates, etc.
Fragments: Alters that are "unfinished" in a way. Often only partially formed.
Collective Name: A name used by the entire system when choosing not to go by individual names.
Collective Pronouns: A set of pronouns used by the entire system when choosing not to go by  individual pronouns.
Roles: What an alter may do in or out of the system, this could be helpful, harmful, or neither.
Fronting: The act of an alter being in control of the body and interacting with the physical world.
Co-Fronting: The act of an alter being in front with another alter, often not being in complete control.
Switching: The act of a fronting alter leaving front and being replaced by another. In disordered systems switches can cause dissociation, amniesia, headaches,blurring of vision, and confusion.
Proxies: A digital tool that allows alters to create a mini account tied to a main account, allowing different alters to use a proxy tag and present as themselves online. A common example of this is Pluralkit and Tupperbox on Discord.
Tulpamancy: A spiritual practice where one creates an alter known as a Tulpa over time and effort.
Willowmancy: A non-spiritual practive where on creates an alter over time and effort. "Willowing" refering to willing an alter into existence. For some this is a quick process, for others it may take years.
### Uncounted People's Stance On Syscourse
Uncounted People is completely netural on syscourse and endogenic systems. There is already not enough scientific research on disordered and traumagenic systems, let alone endogenic systems. We have little to no way of knowing whether endogenic systems do exist or not, and even if they do not, there is something else causing these experiences. You are absolutely allowed to come here and interact with members if you are anti-endo, pro-endo, or anything in between, we just ask that you be respectful. With experts already divided on whether even dissociative disorders are real, everyone in the community needs the support they can get.
